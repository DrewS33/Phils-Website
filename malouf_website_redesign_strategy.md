# PART 1: BRAND & CREATIVE DIRECTION

## Brand Positioning
Malouf Personal Chef occupies the intersection of **private household service and refined culinary craft**. The new brand voice should feel like a trusted recommendation from a friend who happens to know the best private chef in the Triangle — not an ad, not a pitch. Confident, warm, and quietly impressive.

The site must immediately signal to an affluent visitor: *this person belongs in my home.*

## Tone of Voice
- **Confident** — no hedging, no over-explaining
- **Warm but refined** — like a sommelier who makes you feel welcome, not talked down to
- **Specific** — "pan-seared cod with lemon butter" beats "delicious seafood dishes"
- **Understated** — let the craft speak; avoid superlatives and exclamation points
- **Service-minded** — every sentence should orbit the client's experience, not the chef's ego

## Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Background | Warm Ivory / Cream | `#FAF8F4` |
| Section Alt Background | Soft Linen | `#F2EDE6` |
| Primary Text | Deep Charcoal | `#2C2C2C` |
| Secondary Text | Warm Stone | `#6B6560` |
| Accent / CTA | Antique Gold | `#B8954A` |
| Accent Hover | Deep Olive | `#5A6344` |
| Borders / Dividers | Pale Taupe | `#DDD8D0` |
| Dark Section BG | Slate Charcoal | `#1E1E1C` |

## Typography

- **Display / Hero Headline:** Cormorant Garamond (serif) — weight 300–400. Elegant, editorial, luxury-coded.
- **Section Headlines:** Playfair Display (serif) — weight 400–600. Authoritative but approachable.
- **Body / UI Text:** Inter or DM Sans — weight 300–400. Clean, modern, highly readable.
- **Accent / Labels:** Letter-spaced uppercase Inter at 11–12px. Used for section eyebrows and labels.
- **CTA Buttons:** DM Sans Medium, 14px, uppercase, tracked at 0.08em.

## Spacing Philosophy
- Generous breathing room between sections: minimum 100px padding top/bottom on desktop, 64px on mobile.
- Sections should feel like they exhale, not compete.
- A clean rule of thirds guides imagery and copy placement.
- No more than two competing visual focal points per section.

## Imagery Direction
- Large, cinematic food photography with warm, natural lighting.
- No stock images of smiling people holding plates. Instead: textures, ingredients, plating close-ups, hands at work.
- One tasteful portrait of the chef — warm, professional, in the kitchen — anchors the About section.
- Images should feel editorial: think *Bon Appétit* or *Cherry Bombe*, not Groupon.

## Overall UX Philosophy
Single page, anchor-nav driven. The visitor should feel guided, not overwhelmed. Every section earns the scroll to the next. The contact form is always one smooth scroll away from wherever they are.

# PART 2: SINGLE-PAGE SITE MAP (Recommended Section Order)

```
01 ─ NAV BAR            Sticky logo + anchor links + "Book a Consultation" CTA button
02 ─ HERO               Full-bleed image, headline, subhead, two CTAs
03 ─ SERVICES           3-column cards: Meal Prep / Private Dining / Wellness Plans

05 ─ CULINARY STYLE     Editorial food imagery grid + sample menu highlights
06 ─ PRICING            Clean 3-tier card layout with package names
07 ─ ABOUT THE CHEF     2-column: portrait left, bio right
08 ─ TESTIMONIALS       Full-width dark section, 2–3 quotes
09 ─ FAQ                Clean accordion, 6–8 questions
10 ─ CONTACT            2-column: left = messaging/reassurance, right = inquiry form
11 ─ FOOTER             Logo, nav links, social, tagline, copyright
```

**Rationale for this order:**
- Hero earns attention immediately with a strong value statement.
- Services + How It Works educates before asking for commitment.
- Menus + Pricing convert the warm lead who's already intrigued.
- About + Testimonials build trust before the ask.
- FAQ removes final friction before the contact form closes the loop.

# PART 4: DESIGN & LAYOUT IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE

## Navigation Bar
- **Desktop:** Sticky top bar. Logo left. Nav links centered. "Book a Consultation" button right (gold background, white text, rounded pill or slight radius — not fully pill on mobile).
- **Mobile:** Hamburger icon right. Full-screen slide-down or overlay menu with large anchor links. "Book a Consultation" stays visible as a sticky bottom CTA bar on mobile.
- **Behavior:** Nav becomes slightly opaque with a subtle box-shadow on scroll. On the hero, nav starts transparent over the image.
- **Height:** 72px desktop, 60px mobile.

## Hero Section
- **Layout:** Full viewport height (100vh). Full-bleed background image with a dark overlay (gradient from bottom, ~50% black opacity). Text centered or left-aligned on desktop.
- **Desktop:** Eyebrow label top, large display headline (3–4 lines, 72–96px), subhead below (18–20px), two CTAs side by side.
- **Mobile:** Center-aligned. Headline 44–52px. Subhead 16px. CTAs stacked vertically.
- **Image recommendation:** Warm, cinematic overhead prep shot or elegantly plated entrée. Source: Unsplash (search: "fine dining plating" or "chef kitchen elegant").

## Services Section
- **Background:** Warm Ivory (#FAF8F4)
- **Layout:** Eyebrow + headline + subhead centered at top. Three cards in a row on desktop, stacked on mobile.
- **Cards:** Subtle border (1px taupe), soft shadow, 48px padding, no rounded corners (or very slight 4px radius only — keeps it editorial rather than consumer-app). Icon or small image at top of each card. No background fill on cards — let the cream background breathe.
- **CTA Links:** Text links with a →, no button. Keeps it refined.

## How It Works Section
- **Background:** Soft Linen (#F2EDE6) — visually separates from services.
- **Layout:** Three numbered steps in a horizontal row on desktop. On mobile, stack vertically with large step numbers as visual anchors (number in gold, 64px).
- **No cards or borders needed** — let generous padding and the step numbers provide structure.

## Culinary Style / Menus Section
- **Background:** White or Ivory.
- **Layout:** Section headline + subhead centered. Below: two-column layout. Left column = editorial food image (tall, portrait crop). Right column = sample menu list, styled typographically (dish names in serif, day labels in tracked uppercase stone).
- **Special Event menu:** Below the weekly menu in a darker inset box (Linen background) with gold accent headline "Special Event Menu."
- **Mobile:** Single column. Image first, menu below.

## Pricing Section
- **Background:** Warm Ivory.
- **Layout:** Headline + subhead centered. Three cards in a row (desktop), stacked (mobile). Middle card ("Household") gets a subtle gold border or a "Most Popular" label in gold to draw the eye.
- **Card anatomy:** Package name (serif), subtitle (stone, small), price (large, charcoal), feature list (checkmarks in gold), CTA text link.
- **À la carte table:** Clean HTML table below the cards. Minimal lines — top and bottom borders only. No zebra striping.

## About the Chef Section
- **Background:** Soft Linen.
- **Layout:** Two columns, 50/50. Left: portrait image (vertical, 3:4 ratio, subtle drop shadow or warm border). Right: eyebrow + headline + bio paragraphs + CTA link.
- **Mobile:** Image full-width at top, bio below.
- **Portrait placeholder:** Use a warm, editorial stock photo of a chef in a clean apron (Unsplash search: "personal chef kitchen natural light portrait"). Replace with real photo ASAP.

## Testimonials Section
- **Background:** Slate Charcoal (#1E1E1C) — this is the section that should feel the most dramatically different.
- **Layout:** Full-width dark section. Eyebrow in gold. Headline in white serif. 2–3 quote cards or horizontal scroll on mobile.
- **Quote cards:** No hard borders. Large gold quotation mark (decorative, 96px, Cormorant). Quote text in white (18px serif italic). Attribution in stone/muted below.
- **Desktop:** 2–3 quotes side by side or carousel. Mobile: swipeable carousel (use Swiper.js or CSS scroll snap).

## FAQ Section
- **Background:** Ivory.
- **Layout:** Headline centered. Questions in a clean accordion. Each question has a + / − toggle. No borders on the outer container — just a thin separator between items.
- **Typography:** Question in medium charcoal (16px DM Sans), answer in stone (15px).

# PART 5: WHAT TO DELETE, MERGE, AND SIMPLIFY

## Cut Completely
| Current Element | Why to Cut |
|---|---|
| "Affordable" / "budget-friendly" language anywhere | Signals low-end positioning; repels affluent clients |
| "Transform your home into a gourmet paradise" | Cliché, overpromising, generic |
| "For individuals who lack cooking skills" | Condescending to the actual target audience |
| Multiple redundant mentions of "dietary restrictions" | Say it once, say it well — it's already implied by customization |
| "Flavorful, home-cooked meals without the stress" | Weak; undersells the offer |
| Any per-plate or per-pound price framing | Feels transactional rather than premium |
| Separate pages entirely | Consolidated into one scroll-based page |

## Merge These Ideas
- "Culinary Experience" + "Culinary Style" sections → one editorial food/menu section
- The separate testimonials page → integrated into the single-page flow
- "Services" and "How It Works" are complementary but distinct — keep both, but keep them brief

## Rewrite These Phrases
| Current | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "High-quality, home-cooked meals at reasonable prices" | "Restaurant-caliber meals prepared in the comfort of your home" |
| "Affordable fresh meal prepping services" | "Personalized culinary care for your household" |
| "Gourmet paradise" | "Your home, elevated" |
| "Lacking cooking skills or needing time back" | "For households where time is a premium and quality matters" |
| "Delicious meals" | Be specific — name the dish |
| "We accommodate dietary restrictions" | "Every menu is built around your household — restrictions, goals, and preferences included" |

## Pricing Presentation Note
The current per-session pricing ($30–$60) is accurate and honest, but framed as commodity pricing rather than professional service. **Two recommendations:**

1. Present as "Starting at $X per session" — this preserves the floor while implying room for customization.
2. Consider whether pricing is appropriate for the target market long-term. At $30–$60 per session for a personal chef, the implicit value for the client is enormous. There is likely room to increase rates for new clients in the $1M+ household bracket without losing demand. This is a business decision, not a website decision — but the website should never lead with the lowest number.
